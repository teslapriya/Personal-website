About Me

I am Priya Shukla, a passionate astrophysics and electronics enthusiast. I explore the mysteries of the universe through physics and embedded electronics. With a background in machine learning applications in physics, I aim to bridge the gap between technology and space exploration.

My Research

My research focuses on developing innovative instrumentation and methodologies for astronomical exploration. Key areas include:

  • Radio Frequency and Microwave Systems: Developing RF and microwave technologies to enhance the sensitivity and accuracy of astronomical observations.
  • Signal Processing Pipelines: Building robust pipelines to analyze high-volume data from radio telescopes for detecting celestial phenomena like pulsars and fast radio bursts (FRBs).
  • Machine Learning Applications: Applying ML techniques for classification, clustering, and real-time analysis of astronomical data.

Recent Projects

GReX-T3 Subsystem Development

Designed and deployed a subsystem for processing single-pulse candidates, coincidence detection across multiple terminals, and applying ML-based classification to refine astronomical event detection.

Imaging Cracks in Metals with Microwaves

Utilized microwave imaging techniques to study structural defects in metals, demonstrating the potential for non-destructive evaluation in industrial and research applications.
