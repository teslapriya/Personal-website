Research Interest

My primary research focus is on developing technology for space and ground-based astronomical research. In the future, I aspire to be an expert in RF technology along with being skilled in various computational tools

  • Radio Frequency and Microwave Systems: Developing RF and microwave technologies to enhance the sensitivity and accuracy of astronomical observations. Specifically, I am interested in designing circuits and antennas.
  • Signal Processing Pipelines: Building robust pipelines to analyze high-volume data from radio telescopes for detecting celestial phenomena like pulsars and fast radio bursts (FRBs).

Research Work

Galactic Radio Explorer (GReX) Deployment in India and Monitoring of OVRO Station

Focused on monitoring the Owens Valley Radio Observatory Station of GReX, improving the pipeline with digital signal processing and astronomical data analysis techniques, and deploying GReX at GMRT, Ooty, and Gauribidanur Radio Observatory in India.

Multilayer Capacitive Pressure Sensor

Introduced a novel geometric design for multilayered structures to enhance resistance to delamination and reduce surface stress during bending deformation. Explored fabrication using PDMS and PET for capacitive sensors.

Deploying The Galactic Radio Explorer (GReX)

Built front-end modules (FEMs), programmed FEMs and a SNAP board, and addressed thermal and RFI issues for deploying GReX at Owens Valley Radio Observatory (OVRO), California.

Development of the Intel Agilex FPGA Accelerator System

Developed a Simulink-based algorithm for the Square Kilometer Array Radio Telescope to mitigate red noise in real-time processing.

Photometric Analysis and Identification of Young Stellar Objects

Studied the SFO-64 region (an active star-forming region) using NIR data, identifying 156 Young Stellar Objects in the region.
