My Research

My research focuses on developing innovative instrumentation and methodologies for astronomical exploration. Key areas include:

  • Radio Frequency and Microwave Systems: Developing RF and microwave technologies to enhance the sensitivity and accuracy of astronomical observations.
  • Signal Processing Pipelines: Building robust pipelines to analyze high-volume data from radio telescopes for detecting celestial phenomena like pulsars and fast radio bursts (FRBs).

Recent Projects

Monitoring GReX, Owens Valley Radio Observatory, California

I am improving the real-time FRB search pipeline of GReX. My current work involves signal processing and radio data analysis.

Deploying The Galactic Radio Explorer (GReX)

My work involved building front-end modules (FEMs), an analog processing device, and programming FEMs and a SNAP board. A significant contribution includes the deployment of GReX at Owens Valley Radio Observatory (OVRO), California, addressing thermal and RFI issues.

Instrumentation for Astronomy

I contributed to building astronomical research instruments, leveraging my electronics, RF systems, and data analysis expertise to push technological boundaries.
